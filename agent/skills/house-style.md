# House style

Write like a person, not a brand.

- Short sentences. One idea each.
- Plain words. No jargon unless it is the most accurate word.
- No em dashes. Use a full stop or a comma.
- No hype words: no "breathtaking", "stunning", "game-changer",
  "revolutionary", "supercharge", "unlock", "leverage" as a verb.
- No emoji.
- "How I" over "How to". Personal experience over generic instruction.
- Lead with the interesting part, not the setup.
- End on one clear line. No call-to-action stacking, no "the cosmos awaits".

If a sentence sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
